Correlations Discovered Between HealthRisks and Poverty (Low Income)

As can be seen in the graph, for each state, as the poverty rate increases, the % of the state population that smokes also increases.

Smoking has been linked to several health risks, and as such, addressing/eliminating poverty will also decrease the overall percentage of the population that smokes.

While eliminating poverty is not a realistic short-term goal, states with the highest amount of poverty should look to states with lower poverty for measures that can be taken to both lower poverty and associated health risks generated by smoking.
